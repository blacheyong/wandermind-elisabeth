# WanderMind — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
General travelers planning trips — people who want a thoughtful, considered travel experience. They use WanderMind to plan trips that feel personally tailored, not algorithmically generic. Context: deliberate planning, not impulse booking. They take their time. The interface should not rush them.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: Clear, neat, perfect.**

WanderMind is a product of BYCo (Blache & Young Consulting). The brand reference is blacheyong.com — exact match required. BYCo is a premium, restrained brand: confident without being loud, editorial without being cold. No playfulness, no startup energy. Just precision.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Primary reference**: blacheyong.com — replicate the brand exactly
- **Visual tone**: Editorial minimalism. Large, unhurried typography. Generous whitespace. Controlled, high-contrast palette.
- **Typeface**: SegmentAlt (available at `public/byc-2.0-master/src/fonts/segment-*.woff2`) — Bold, SemiBold, Medium, Regular. PPNeueMontreal and PPTelegraf also available for pairing if needed.
- **Color**: Primary blue `#2d39f7` as the single accent. Otherwise near-black text on white/off-white backgrounds. No color noise.
- **Theme**: Light mode only.
- **Anti-reference**: Nothing too small. Text must breathe. No cramped layouts, no tiny labels, no 10px fine print.
- **Quality bar**: Client-facing flagship. Every pixel must be intentional.

### Design Principles

1. **BYCo fidelity first** — If it wouldn't appear on blacheyong.com, it doesn't belong here. When in doubt, remove rather than add.

2. **Generous scale** — Text sizes, line heights, and padding should err on the side of too large rather than too small. This creates calm. Cramped = anxious. Spacious = confident.

3. **Typographic hierarchy over decoration** — All visual hierarchy comes from size, weight, and spacing. No decorative borders, no colored background pills, no icons-next-to-every-heading.

4. **One accent, used sparingly** — `#2d39f7` is the only color. It marks the active state, the primary action, and focus. Everywhere else: greys and near-black.

5. **States must be complete** — Every interactive element has a default, hover, focus, active, and disabled state. A half-implemented component is not flagship quality.
